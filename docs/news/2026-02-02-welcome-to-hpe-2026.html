Welcome to HPE 2026

Published: 02 Feb 2026

Dear Year 9 Parents and Caregivers, 

Welcome to Health and Physical Education (HPE) for 2026! 

We are excited to be working with your child as they continue their HPE journey this year. It has been wonderful to see students returning with enthusiasm, ready to take on new challenges and continue building their skills and knowledge. 

This year’s program offers a variety of practical units, including Futsal, Oztag and Touch Football, Softball, Golf, and Long Life Physical Activity. Our health units will explore important topics such as Positive Relationships and Risky Business, encouraging students to reflect on personal development, decision-making, and wellbeing. 

 

Excursions – A Highlight of Year 9 

Year 9 also offers several excursion opportunities, designed to connect classroom learning with real-world experiences and foster engagement with our local community. 

Excursions are considered a privilege, and students must meet school expectations to participate fully. A key requirement for all off-campus activities is wearing the full PE uniform, which reflects the school’s values and ensures students are easily identifiable for safety reasons. This expectation will be clearly communicated to students, allowing them to take responsibility for their preparation and organisation. 

 

Setting Up for Success in HPE 

To get the most from this year’s program, students are expected to: 

  • Follow the uniform policy by wearing the correct PE uniform on scheduled days and for excursions (check Nexus for your child’s timetable). 

  • Pack their PE uniform the day before and set reminders for upcoming excursions. 

  • Bring a water bottle and hat for all practical lessons. 

  • Be prompt to class, change quickly, and participate to the best of their ability. 

  • Practise skills outside of class to continue their development. 

 

Uniform Requirements and Consequences 

Students are expected to meet PE uniform requirements, including on excursion days. Staff will support students in developing strategies for staying organised. The following process will apply for uniform breaches: 

  • 1st Offence: Warning conversation 

  • 2nd Offence: Detention issued through Nexus 

  • 3rd Offence: Detention issued through Nexus and contact home 

  • 4th Offence: Involvement of the Head of Department 

 

Teacher Contact Details 

If you have any questions or concerns, particularly regarding upcoming excursions, please don’t hesitate to contact your child’s teacher through Nexus or via email: caitliln.hackett@radford.act.edu.au

 

We look forward to a year full of engaging learning experiences, supporting every student to develop their skills, confidence, and connection with the wider community. 

 

Kind regards,

Caitlin Hackett
